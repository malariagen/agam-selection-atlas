H12 / Guinea-Bissau / Chromosome 3 / #3Â¶

This page describes a signal of selection found in the Guinea Bissau population using the H12 (Garud et al. 20XX) statistic.The inferred focus of this signal is on chromosome arm 3L between positions 25,080,001 and 25,120,000. The evidence supporting this signal is weak ( < 50 on one or both flanks).

Signal location. Blue markers show the values of the selection statistic. The dashed black line shows the fitted peak model. The shaded red area shows the focus of the selection signal. The shaded blue area shows the genomic region in linkage with the selection event. Use the mouse wheel or the controls at the right of the plot to zoom in, and hover over genes to see gene names and descriptions.

Gene AGAP011480 overlaps the focal region.

The following 4 genes are within 50 kbp of the focal region: AGAP011475 (Envelysin), AGAP011477 (eupolytin), AGAP011478, AGAP011479 (hyaluronoglucosaminidase).
