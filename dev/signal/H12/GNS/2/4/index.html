H12 / Guinea An. gambiae / Chromosome 2 / #4Â¶

This page describes a signal of selection found in the Guinea An. gambiae population using the H12 (Garud et al. 20XX) statistic.The inferred focus of this signal is on chromosome arm 2L between positions 25,660,001 and 25,760,000. The evidence supporting this signal is moderate ( >= 50 on both flanks).

Signal location. Blue markers show the values of the selection statistic. The dashed black line shows the fitted peak model. The shaded red area shows the focus of the selection signal. The shaded blue area shows the genomic region in linkage with the selection event. Use the mouse wheel or the controls at the right of the plot to zoom in, and hover over genes to see gene names and descriptions.

The following 7 genes overlap the focal region: AGAP006052 (protein phosphatase 1, regulatory (inhibitor) subunit 3), AGAP006053, AGAP006054, AGAP006055 (NF-kappa-B inhibitor-like protein 2), AGAP006056, AGAP006057 (multiple coagulation factor deficiency 2), AGAP006058 (heparan sulfate 2-o-sulfotransferase).

The following 8 genes are within 50 kbp of the focal region: AGAP006045 (protein yorkie), AGAP006046 (Med23 - mediator of RNA polymerase II transcription subunit 23), AGAP006047 (CYP4J9 - cytochrome P450), AGAP006048 (CYP4J5 - cytochrome P450), AGAP006049 (CYP4J10 - cytochrome P450), AGAP006050 (AarF domain containing kinase 5), AGAP006051 (pentatricopeptide repeat domain 1), AGAP006059.
