H12 / Burkina Faso An. coluzzii / Chromosome 3 / #2Â¶

This page describes a signal of selection found in the Burkina Faso An. coluzzii population using the H12 (Garud et al. 20XX) statistic.The inferred focus of this signal is on chromosome arm 3L between positions 11,200,001 and 11,240,000. The evidence supporting this signal is weak ( < 50 on one or both flanks).

Signal location. Blue markers show the values of the selection statistic. The dashed black line shows the fitted peak model. The shaded red area shows the focus of the selection signal. The shaded blue area shows the genomic region in linkage with the selection event. Use the mouse wheel or the controls at the right of the plot to zoom in, and hover over genes to see gene names and descriptions.

The following 2 genes overlap the focal region: AGAP010815 (TEP1 - thioester-containing protein 1), AGAP010816 (TEP3 - thioester-containing protein 3).

The following 4 genes are within 50 kbp of the focal region: AGAP010813 (TEP18 - thioester-containing protein 18), AGAP010814 (TEP6 - thioester-containing protein 6), AGAP010817, AGAP010818 (TEP11 - thioester-containing protein 11).
